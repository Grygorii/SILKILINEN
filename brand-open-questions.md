# SILKILINEN — Brand: Open Questions

Companion to `brand.md`. These are **undecided**. Don't write copy or build features that assume a particular answer until real data is in.

## The customer reality gap
- The volume product on Etsy is **silk panties**.
- The visible products on silkilinen.com are dresses and robes.
- The strategic work to date assumed a "32-year-old slow-fashion buyer with €200 budget for silk slip dresses" (persona: Maeve).
- These two pictures don't match. **We don't know who the real customer is yet.**

**Action gating:** before writing positioning copy, persona-driven product descriptions, or "for the [adjective] woman who [verb]"-style hero text, pull real Etsy data: units per month, average price, top items, repeat rate, geographic distribution.

## Positioning options on the table
- **A.** Luxury slip-dress / loungewear brand (matches what's visible on the site, contradicts sales data)
- **B.** Direct-to-consumer silk basics at volume (panties + camisoles + simple slips, matches sales data)
- **C.** Hybrid: hero basics fund hero pieces (basics drive cash, dresses build brand)
- **D.** Something else after seeing the data

Each option implies different copy voice, photography direction, price architecture, and email cadence. Don't pick one in a Claude chat. Pick it from the data.
